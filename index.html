Civil Rights & Social Justice

National Civil Rights Museum - Memphis, TN

Growing up I was always inspired by the great civil rights icon Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. One of my most memorable moments had to be the day that I visited the the Lorraine Motel, the very room where King lodged in preperation for the Sanitation Workers' Strike and became the site where he was assasinated on April 4, 1968. Social Justice and the fight for civil rights remain important to me as an adult, shaping my goals and aspirations even now.
